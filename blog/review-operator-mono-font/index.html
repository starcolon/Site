Review! Operator Mono Font
6 Nov 2016 | Tao P.R.

Most Expensive Font I've ever bought

I know how crazy to reveal I bought this stunning font at $179, yes, just for coding. I'm a guy who rotate fonts for my editor around so often. So, it's not too surprising I'm a big fan of good paid fonts!

**I bought a small subset of the whole family.
The full set is somewhat more expensive.

How it looks on my editor

The font does not disappoint me at all. It just looks amazing on my Sublime Text!

"Glyphs are very crispy and clear."

"Non-Alphabet symbols are adorable."

"I love how slant brackets, ampersands, etc. look."

Terminal, still looks so clear

Usually, texts on Terminal screen may look so complicated. Poor-designed fonts may probably pollute things much worse. But not Operator.

"It is highly readable and tidy."

Jaw-Dropping Italic

Its unique italic glyphs say everything it is. I try a grotesk look on my Jupyter notebook and that makes my day.

"Grotesque, classy coding!"

Will It Worth $179?

Definitely. I had an experience with 4~5 paid monospaced font packs which cost around $30 each. I would say from my gut feeling that it outclasses all the font families I had.

Bottom Line

This review was written based on my sole opinion.
All individuals have different taste so YMMV.
