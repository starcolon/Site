Basic guide: Planet of Cells

Enter the planet, explore

You are welcome to join the planet. All sign up process is free and it only needs your desired display name with an email adress and that's it!
In Planet of Cells, cells are threatening. You right click anywhere on the world map to spawn a cell. You will see a blue cell surrounded by a green circle which denotes the potential territory of it. As time goes, the cell keeps dividing, sometimes mutating so the number of the population in a territory becomes bigger so the territory expands.

Red and green

All your territories are drawn in green while other foreign territories are all in red, no matter whose they are. Beware of getting your territories overlapped by foreign territory which is much larger. We'll show you next why.

Grow and expand your empire

As time goes on, your every single cell keeps reproducing and slowly expands their territory. However, it only grows up and expands while you are in the game (keep your browser window open so they continuously develop).
There are some possibilities of having some cell species mutating while reproducing. The mutated cells tend to live longer and reproduce more than the primitive cell types. So having more mutated cells in your territory will likely expand the area slightly faster. Players cannot have a grip on the mutation rate, nevertheless. The process is 100% natural.

Gain more power

What is power in Planet of Cells? Both Budget (coin) and Reputation point make one player different to others. Similar to the real world, you can use coin for buying things yet keep in mind that everything in the game is much expensive. We recommend you to use it with care. You can buy items to threathen other player's territories or expand your own.
Reputation point indicates a player's degree of influence. Basically, a player can spawn his cell anywhere in the no man's land or his own territory. To spawn a cell in other player's territory, you need to have more reputation point than the owner of such that territory. Moreover, more powerful items require more reputation points to activate thus only experienced players can make use of those dominant weapons.
Gaining coin in Planet of Cells is not an easy job. Every 49th and 149th territory you create within one day (UTC time) rewards you 3,000 and 60,000 coin respectively. The other ways to gain more coin is completing missions.
To grow your reputation point which explicitly represents your influence, you have to show how influential your empire is. The simplest way to gain reputation point is merge multiple territories together by creating many territories which are really close to each other. Sibling territories will merge into one and you gain reputation point for free.
Merging your own territories together reward you some reputation point. However, merging your territories with other's reward you even much more. All you have to do is grow surround foreign territory with your own, grow them large. Once it significantly overpowers other foreign territory, red territory becomes yours and rewards you some good reputation point. Some players choose to surround red territories and keep the browser open for a good few hours, this is also an insane method to take control over other player's territory.

Invasion!

As mentioned in the previous chapter, you can merge your own territory with other player's territories and take control over them absolutely by surround red territories with your green ones. The simple rules of thumb are:
  • Overnumber opponent's territories by number of circular territory
  • Overnumber opponent's territories by the size in total (locally)
  • Overnumber opponent's territories by the number of population in total

Destructive items

Planet of Cells hand you a set of destructive powers through item shop. You can buy it anytime you want with coin. Remember that coin is not easy resource so use it with care. Items are mostly expensive, so let's say destructive power is costly.
More expensive items tend to destroy more cells and come with more lethal power.
Only nukes can destroy a weak territory while vaccines can only reduce the population size. Unstable P1 vaccine is also a very fun item to try, its effect ranges from very poor killer to extremely destructive. It depends on your luck.
To use an item on a territory, follow these few steps:
  • Click on a territory you want to do something with it
  • As the popup window appears, click `Use item’
  • Pick an item you want to use, that's it!
